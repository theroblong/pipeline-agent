# Loud Security Customer Operations Packet

## Internal Objective

Prepare for a first practical AI and automation conversation with Loud Security. The goal is to help John and Brian see concrete use cases without making the conversation feel abstract or overly technical.

## Recommended Opening Angle

Lead with practical customer operations automation, not "AI" in the abstract. The conversation should show how connected customer data, service workflow visibility, installed asset context, and selective automation can create a clearer operating layer for the business.

## Use Cases To Discuss

### Customer Follow-Up Automation

- Identify customers who are ready for proactive outreach.
- Trigger post-install and post-service check-ins.
- Route follow-ups by customer type, location, issue, or sales status.
- Track whether outreach was completed and what happened next.

### Service Ticketing And Resolution Visibility

- Capture calls, service issues, and resolution time.
- Track open items by customer, location, installer, and equipment type.
- Create alerts for stalled service work.
- Measure response time and repeat issue rate.

### Installed Asset And Location Tracking

- Store what was installed, where, when, by whom, and under what service status.
- Let support or installation teams quickly answer customer-specific equipment questions.
- Create a foundation for a customer portal or mobile app later.

### Customer Portal Possibility

- Let customers see service history, installed equipment, scheduled visits, and support status.
- Start with the data and workflow foundation before defining portal scope.

## Offer Fit

- Automation Consulting Services: best first fit for follow-up, ticketing, and service workflow.
- Aevah: possible data layer for customer, location, installation, and service records.
- Product Development And Intelligent Systems: possible portal or app build after workflow discovery.

## Suggested Meeting Agenda

1. Which customer lifecycle workflow would be most valuable to make faster, clearer, or more consistent?
2. What systems hold customer, service, installation, and location information today?
3. Where would automated follow-up or service visibility create measurable operating value?
4. Which workflow is the best candidate for a first scoped pilot?
5. What would a useful 30 to 60 day pilot need to prove?

## Human Review Needed

- Confirm stakeholder names and roles.
- Confirm whether ServIT, Pruvida, or Aevah branding should be used.
- Verify current systems before suggesting integration paths.
