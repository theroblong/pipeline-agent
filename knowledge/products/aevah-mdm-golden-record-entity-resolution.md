# Aevah MDM Golden Record And Entity Resolution

## Product Summary

This Aevah offer helps enterprises resolve fragmented customer, product, supplier, location, and operational records into trusted golden records with lineage and survivorship.

## Best Fit

- Target accounts: Enterprises with duplicate, conflicting, or fragmented entity records across ERP, CRM, billing, procurement, logistics, or data platforms.
- Target buyers: CDO, CIO, data governance leaders, enterprise architects, operations leaders.
- Target departments: Data governance, IT, operations, procurement, supply chain, finance, sales operations.
- Best-fit buying-cycle stages: `problem-exploration`, `active-evaluation`, `solution-shaping`, `proposal-procurement`.
- Common entry points: MDM modernization, product/SKU harmonization, customer matching, supplier normalization, AI data foundation.

## Pain Signals

- The same customer, supplier, product, SKU, or location appears differently across systems.
- Reporting and analytics cannot agree on basic entity identity.
- Matching rules are manual or poorly governed.
- Business users do not trust master data.
- Entity errors break downstream processes.
- There is no clear lineage from golden record back to source systems.

## Outcomes And Value

- Trusted golden records for key business entities.
- Clear lineage and survivorship rules.
- Better reporting, analytics, AI, and operational workflows.
- Reduced duplicate records, disputes, and manual reconciliation.
- A stronger foundation for governed data products.

## Positioning

### Short Positioning

Aevah helps companies answer "what is this thing?" across disconnected systems, then maintain that answer as the business changes.

### Customer-Facing Positioning

Aevah can help your team resolve fragmented entity records into governed golden records, with matching, lineage, survivorship, and stewardship built into the operating model.

### Discovery Positioning

If the prospect has duplicate or conflicting records across systems, position this offer around identity resolution and business trust before broader analytics or AI claims.

## Qualification Questions

- Where do you have duplicate or conflicting records?
- Which entities matter most: customers, products, suppliers, locations, assets, employees, or something else?
- How many systems create or modify those records?
- Who decides which value survives when systems disagree?
- What business process breaks when identity is wrong?
- How do you trace a golden record back to source systems?
- Are matching rules manually managed today?
- Do business users trust the current master data?
