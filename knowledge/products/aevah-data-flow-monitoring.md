# Aevah Data Flow Monitoring

## Product Summary

Aevah Data Flow Monitoring gives enterprises operational visibility into how data moves, changes, fails, and performs across systems.

## Best Fit

- Target accounts: Organizations with business-critical integrations, data pipelines, event streams, reports, or operational feeds.
- Target buyers: CIO, CDO, VP Operations, enterprise architects, support and operations leaders.
- Target departments: IT, data, support operations, analytics, enterprise architecture, operations.
- Best-fit buying-cycle stages: `problem-exploration`, `active-evaluation`, `solution-shaping`.
- Common entry points: Pipeline failures, unreliable reports, data quality incidents, integration outages, slow incident diagnosis.

## Pain Signals

- Teams discover data issues only after a user complains.
- Reports, dashboards, or downstream feeds need clearer root-cause visibility.
- Data quality changes are not monitored as data moves.
- Critical integrations lack health, freshness, latency, throughput, or error visibility.
- Support teams spend too much time investigating data incidents.

## Outcomes And Value

- Faster detection and diagnosis of data-flow issues.
- Better visibility into quality, lineage, and operational health.
- Reduced firefighting across data and integration teams.
- More reliable reporting, analytics, AI, and operational workflows.
- Clearer ownership for business-critical data flows.

## Positioning

### Short Positioning

Aevah Data Flow Monitoring gives enterprises real-time visibility into data movement, quality, lineage, failures, and operational performance.

### Customer-Facing Positioning

Aevah can help your team see where data flows are healthy, stale, broken, or risky before downstream reports, processes, or business users are affected.

### Discovery Positioning

If the prospect describes data incidents, unreliable feeds, or integration firefighting, position this offer around observability and operational reliability.

## Qualification Questions

- Where do data-flow failures show up today?
- How do you know when a data pipeline or integration is unhealthy?
- Who gets blamed when reports, dashboards, or operational feeds are wrong?
- Do you have visibility into data quality as data moves?
- Can you trace failures back to source systems or transformation steps?
- Which integrations are business-critical?
- Do you measure latency, freshness, throughput, and error rates?
- How much manual effort is spent investigating data issues?
