# Automation Consulting Services

## Product Summary

Automation consulting services help teams remove repetitive work, connect systems, design workflows, and implement reliable automations across business operations.

## Best Fit

- Target accounts: Organizations with manual operations, fragmented systems, recurring process bottlenecks, or high-volume administrative work.
- Target buyers: Operations leaders, process owners, technology leaders, finance leaders, service leaders.
- Target departments: Operations, finance, customer operations, sales operations, service delivery, IT.
- Best-fit pipeline stages: Discovery, solution fit, proposal.
- Best-fit buying-cycle stages: `problem-exploration`, `active-evaluation`, `solution-shaping`, `proposal-procurement`.
- Common entry points: Manual handoffs, spreadsheet workflows, duplicate entry, disconnected tools, recurring reports, approval workflows.

## Pain Signals

- People copy data between systems.
- Teams rely on spreadsheets for recurring operational work.
- Work stalls at handoffs or approvals.
- Reporting takes too much time.
- Process exceptions are tracked manually.
- Systems exist but do not work together well.

## Outcomes And Value

- Reduced manual work.
- Faster cycle times.
- Better process consistency.
- Fewer handoff errors.
- Improved visibility into workflow status.
- More capacity for higher-value work.

## Positioning

### Short Positioning

Automation consulting services help teams remove repetitive work and connect workflows across systems.

### Customer-Facing Positioning

Pruvida can help your team identify automation-ready workflows, design more reliable operating paths, and implement automation that improves speed, consistency, and visibility.

### Discovery Positioning

If the pain sounds like repeated manual steps, disconnected tools, or preventable handoff delays, automation consulting may be the most direct path to value.

## Qualification Questions

- Which tasks are repeated most often?
- Where does work slow down or get re-entered?
- Which systems need to exchange information?
- What errors or delays happen because the process is manual?
- What volume, frequency, or labor cost makes this worth automating?
