# Aevah AI-Ready Data And Agentic Semantic Layer

## Product Summary

This Aevah offer creates the trusted semantic and operational data layer required for enterprise AI agents to reason over business data safely.

## Best Fit

- Target accounts: Enterprises pursuing AI agents, AI assistants, or AI-enabled analytics but lacking trusted operational data.
- Target buyers: CIO, CDO, VP Data, AI leaders, enterprise architects, data governance leaders.
- Target departments: Data, IT, analytics, enterprise architecture, AI teams, governance.
- Best-fit buying-cycle stages: `signal`, `problem-exploration`, `active-evaluation`, `solution-shaping`.
- Common entry points: AI pilot stalls, inconsistent business definitions, lineage gaps, AI governance concerns.

## Pain Signals

- AI pilots can generate text but cannot reliably understand enterprise data.
- Data definitions vary across teams or systems.
- Lineage, freshness, auditability, or explainability is missing.
- Humans still reconcile data before AI or analytics can use it.
- Leaders want AI agents but cannot trust the underlying business context.

## Outcomes And Value

- Better AI-readiness for enterprise data.
- Trusted semantic layer for agents and analytics.
- More explainable and auditable AI answers.
- Reduced risk of agents reasoning over stale, conflicting, or poorly defined data.
- Clearer connection between AI use cases and governed data products.

## Positioning

### Short Positioning

Aevah gives enterprise AI agents the trusted data foundation they need to understand customers, products, operations, events, and business context.

### Customer-Facing Positioning

Aevah can help your AI initiatives move beyond generic language generation by giving agents governed access to trusted business data, consistent definitions, lineage, and operational context.

### Discovery Positioning

If AI interest is real but the blocker is trusted data, business meaning, or explainability, position this Aevah motion before or alongside AI application work.

## Qualification Questions

- What AI use cases are blocked by data trust issues?
- What systems would an AI agent need to understand your business?
- Are business terms consistently defined across teams?
- How do you prevent AI from answering with stale or incorrect data?
- Where do humans still interpret or reconcile data before it can be used?
- Do AI answers need to be explainable or auditable?
