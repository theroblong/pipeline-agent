# Aevah

## Product Summary

Aevah is an enterprise intelligence platform that helps companies turn fragmented operational data into trusted, governed, AI-ready data products. It combines data integration, semantic modeling, data quality, lineage, governance, analytics, and AI-assisted workflow into a business-facing platform.

## Best Fit

- Target accounts: Enterprises with valuable data spread across ERP systems, spreadsheets, legacy applications, SaaS platforms, data warehouses, and operational workflows.
- Target buyers: CIO, CDO, VP Data, VP IT, COO, CFO, business transformation leaders, enterprise architects, data governance leaders.
- Target departments: IT, data, analytics, operations, finance, governance, enterprise architecture.
- Best-fit pipeline stages: Discovery, solution fit, pilot shaping.
- Best-fit buying-cycle stages: `problem-exploration`, `active-evaluation`, `solution-shaping`.
- Common entry points: Data product strategy, AI readiness, MDM modernization, entity resolution, data observability, governed analytics, operational intelligence.

## Pain Signals

- Valuable data is trapped across ERP, spreadsheets, legacy applications, SaaS platforms, and warehouses.
- The business needs trusted answers faster than IT delivery cycles can support.
- Data is inconsistent, poorly governed, or difficult to reconcile.
- AI use cases are blocked because underlying data is not trusted.
- Reporting, analytics, or operational decisions still depend on manual extracts.
- Lineage, auditability, stewardship, or semantic consistency is missing.

## Outcomes And Value

- Trusted, governed, AI-ready data products.
- Reduced integration and reporting backlog.
- Better data quality, lineage, stewardship, and semantic consistency.
- Faster business access to usable operational data.
- Stronger foundation for analytics, AI agents, and operational applications.
- More confidence in metrics, decisions, and business workflows.

## Positioning

### Short Positioning

Aevah helps enterprises convert disconnected operational data into trusted, governed, AI-ready data products that business teams can use without waiting on long IT delivery cycles.

### Customer-Facing Positioning

Aevah can help your team define, govern, enrich, and operationalize trusted data products that support analytics, AI agents, applications, and business workflows without disrupting core systems.

### Discovery Positioning

If the issue is fragmented operational data, inconsistent definitions, manual reporting, data-quality risk, or AI-readiness, Aevah may be the core platform motion.

## Qualification Questions

- Where does your business struggle to get a trusted answer from data today?
- Which operational systems are hardest to combine or reconcile?
- What reports or decisions still depend on spreadsheets or manual extracts?
- Where do data quality issues create rework, disputes, or lack of confidence?
- What AI use cases are blocked because the underlying data is not trusted?
- Who owns data definitions today, and how are changes governed?
- How long does it typically take to create a new reporting or analytics data set?
- Which systems are too risky or expensive to modify directly?
- Where do you need lineage, auditability, or explainability?
- What business process would improve first if your data were trusted and accessible?

## Objections And Responses

| Objection | Response | Proof Needed |
| --- | --- | --- |
| We already have a data warehouse. | Aevah does not need to replace the warehouse. It can help define, govern, enrich, and operationalize trusted data products that feed warehouses, lakes, BI tools, AI agents, and applications. | Current warehouse role, data-product gaps, and governance needs. |
| We already have integration tools. | Aevah should be positioned above raw integration: meaning, quality, lineage, survivorship, governance, and business usability. | Current integration architecture and unmet governance/usability needs. |
| We are not ready for AI. | That may be the reason to start. Aevah can help prepare operational data so AI can be used more safely and effectively. | AI use cases, data readiness, and governance concerns. |
| This sounds like MDM. | Aevah can include MDM-like capabilities while extending into semantic data products, analytics, workflow, and AI enablement. | Entity scope, governance requirements, and downstream use cases. |
| Our data is too messy. | Aevah should be positioned for messy, distributed enterprise data where scoring, reconciliation, lineage, and governance are part of the value. | Data-quality profile and priority entities or workflows. |
