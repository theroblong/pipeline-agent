## Observations

### 2026-06-02: Acquisitive Enterprise Portal Fragmentation

- Situation: Enterprise has acquired many companies and now has fragmented portals, data, and customer workflows.
- Offer positioned: Aevah, Aevah MDM Golden Record And Entity Resolution, Aevah Automated Migration Tool.
- Why it appeared to fit: The pain is data unification, customer workflow speed, entity consistency, and integration across acquired systems.
- Evidence: Fiserv transcript thread described many acquired companies, multiple portals, customer UX pain, and a need to get data in one place.
- Outcome: unknown.
- Confidence: medium.
- Related accounts: `knowledge/accounts/fiserv.md`
- Related opportunities: `knowledge/opportunities/fiserv-data-unification-portal-consolidation.md`
- Follow-up question: Which workflow and data domain should be used as the first paid pilot?

### 2026-06-02: Field Logistics Cost Visibility

- Situation: High-volume field operations have driver dwell time, idle fuel cost, overtime, route timing, and turnover pain.
- Offer positioned: Aevah plus Automation Consulting Services.
- Why it appeared to fit: The prospect needs root-cause visibility and workflow automation, not only static tracking.
- Evidence: RaceTrac transcript thread described driver stop duration, idle fuel cost, overtime, food cost, and onboarding delays.
- Outcome: unknown.
- Confidence: medium.
- Related accounts: `knowledge/accounts/racetrac.md`
- Related opportunities: `knowledge/opportunities/racetrac-operational-intelligence-workforce-automation.md`
- Follow-up question: What data exists today for route, stop, truck, driver, fuel, food cost, and onboarding processes?

### 2026-06-02: Service Business Customer Operations Gaps

- Situation: Service business has grown quickly but has weak customer follow-up, service tracking, and installed asset records.
- Offer positioned: Automation Consulting Services, Aevah, Product Development And Intelligent Systems.
- Why it appeared to fit: Customer operations can be improved through workflow automation, centralized data, ticketing, and possible portal/app work.
- Evidence: Loud Security transcript thread described poor follow-up, weak data system, customer service gaps, installed asset tracking, and portal possibilities.
- Outcome: unknown.
- Confidence: medium.
- Related accounts: `knowledge/accounts/loud-security.md`
- Related opportunities: `knowledge/opportunities/loud-security-customer-operations-automation.md`
- Follow-up question: Is the first value path customer follow-up, service ticketing, installed asset tracking, or customer portal?
