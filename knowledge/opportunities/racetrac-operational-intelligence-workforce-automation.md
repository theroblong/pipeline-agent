## Problem And Value

- Problem: RaceTrac appears to lack sufficient visibility into why driver stops take too long, how route and idle-time cost add up, how food cost should be forecast, and how staffing/onboarding delays affect labor capacity.
- Business impact: Fuel waste, overtime, slower routes, turnover cost, delayed start dates, lower operational predictability, and food cost leakage.
- Desired outcome: Improve operational visibility and reduce preventable cost across logistics, food cost, and workforce processes.
- Value hypothesis: Even small reductions in stop time, idle time, onboarding delay, and staffing markup could produce meaningful savings at fleet/store scale.
- Success criteria: Needs discovery.

## Discovery Questions

- What are the top 3 logistics metrics RaceTrac is trying to improve?
- How is driver stop time measured today, and what root causes are visible versus unknown?
- What is the current average stop duration and target duration?
- What is the cost model for idle fuel, overtime, route delays, and driver turnover?
- Which systems hold route, stop, truck, fuel, store, and delivery data?
- What food cost signals matter most: commodity cost, store demand, waste, storage, pricing, or stockouts?
- How long does onboarding take by role, and which steps create delays?
- What staffing company markup, turnover, or contractor-to-permanent conversion data is available?
- What would a 90-day pilot need to prove?
